Famous Marvals of Nagaland

Kohima War Cemetery

The Kohima War Cemetery is a historic memorial located in Kohima, Nagaland, dedicated to the soldiers of the Allied Forces who died in the Battle of Kohima during World War II (1944). Set on the slopes of Garrison Hill, the cemetery holds over 1,420 graves of brave soldiers, including Indians, British, and others. At the center lies the famous epitaph: “When You Go Home, Tell Them of Us and Say, For Your Tomorrow, We Gave Our Today.” Surrounded by serene greenery, it is a symbol of sacrifice, peace, and remembrance, visited by people from around the world.

Japfu Peak

Japfu Peak is the second-highest mountain in Nagaland, standing at 3,048 meters above sea level. Located near Kohima, it is part of the Dzüko Valley range and is a popular destination for trekkers and nature lovers. The peak is famous for being home to the world’s tallest rhododendron tree, which blooms beautifully during spring. From the top, visitors can enjoy panoramic views of valleys, forests, and far-off hills. Japfu Peak is not just a scenic marvel but also a symbol of Nagaland’s natural beauty and biodiversity.
