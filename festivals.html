Famous Festivals of Uttar Pradesh

Diwali

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is one of the most celebrated and grand festivals in Uttar Pradesh, especially in cities like Ayodhya, Varanasi, and Lucknow. Symbolizing the victory of light over darkness and good over evil, it marks the return of Lord Ram to Ayodhya after 14 years of exile and his triumph over the demon king Ravana. In Ayodhya, the celebration is breathtaking. The city lights up with lakhs of earthen diyas during “Deepotsav”, setting world records. The banks of the Saryu River glow as the air fills with chants, firecrackers, and festive joy. People clean and decorate their homes with rangoli, lamps, and flowers, worship Goddess Lakshmi and Lord Ganesha, and share sweets with family and neighbors. Markets buzz with color, and temples sparkle with devotion. In Varanasi, the Ganga Aarti during Diwali becomes even more majestic, with floating lamps and fireworks lighting up the holy river. Diwali in Uttar Pradesh is not just a festival—it is a spiritual, visual, and emotional experience that brings communities together in a celebration of light, faith, and heritage.

Holi

Holi, the festival of colors, is celebrated with unmatched energy and devotion in Uttar Pradesh, especially in Mathura, Vrindavan, Barsana, Nandgaon, and Lucknow. It marks the arrival of spring, the victory of good over evil, and the divine love of Radha and Krishna. In Mathura and Vrindavan, Holi becomes a spiritual spectacle. Temples come alive with music, dance, and color-splashing rituals. Devotees chant bhajans, throw gulal (colored powder), and reenact Krishna’s playful pastimes with Radha and the gopis. One of the most unique traditions is Lathmar Holi in Barsana and Nandgaon, where women playfully hit men with sticks while singing and dancing—a joyful reenactment of Radha-Krishna’s teasing moments. In cities like Lucknow and Varanasi, Holi is celebrated with color throwing, sweets like gujiya, thandai (often laced with bhang), and cultural gatherings. It is a day when social boundaries blur, and people unite in laughter, music, and vibrant celebration. Holi in Uttar Pradesh is more than just a festival—it’s a colorful expression of devotion, unity, and the eternal bond of love and joy.
